# WORLD_BUILDING

Version: Phase 1

Project: Osaider

Engine: Unreal Engine 5.6

---

# 1. WORLD PREMISE

Osaider is a floating palace above the ocean.

It is not only a palace.

It is also:

* A nation
* A factory
* A military system
* A historical archive
* A machine for producing ideology

The people living inside the palace believe that they are protecting their homeland from an external enemy called Osaider.

However, as the player explores the palace, they gradually discover that the war may be a constructed narrative.

The enemy may not exist.

The palace may be fighting against an idea, a memory, or a fabricated threat.

---

# 2. THE ENDLESS CYCLE

The daily life of the palace is organized around a repeated cycle.

During the day:

* Citizens work inside the palace.
* Weapons are produced.
* Documents are processed.
* Historical records are edited.
* Official memories are maintained.

During the night:

* The produced weapons are sent downward.
* The weapons are melted in the underwater steelworks.
* The raw material returns to the system.
* The next day begins again.

This creates a closed loop:

Production

↓

Destruction

↓

Recycling

↓

Production Again

The system does not exist to win a war.

The system exists to continue itself.

---

# 3. THE PALACE

The palace is both a symbolic and functional space.

It represents:

* Authority
* Memory
* Bureaucracy
* Ritual
* Production
* Surveillance

Architecturally, the palace should feel like a combination of:

* A sacred political monument
* A government archive
* A factory
* A machine
* A theatrical set
* A floating ruin

The player should feel that the palace is ancient, powerful, and unstable.

---

# 4. SOCIETY

The citizens of the palace live under a dual identity.

They are both:

* Citizens
* Soldiers

This dual identity is important.

The palace does not need a separate army because every citizen is already part of the military system.

Work, memory, obedience, and production are all treated as patriotic duties.

---

# 5. THE WAR

The official narrative says:

The palace is under constant threat from Osaider.

The people must continue producing weapons to protect their homeland.

The Archivists maintain the legitimacy of this war through documents, ceremonies, records, and public narratives.

However, hidden evidence suggests:

* The enemy may be fictional.
* The war may have ended long ago.
* The war may never have happened.
* Osaider may be a name used to justify endless control.

The player should never receive a single final truth too early.

The uncertainty is part of the narrative structure.

---

# 6. FACTIONS

## 6.1 Archivist

Color:

Red

Role:

Official authority.

The Archivists control the records of the palace.

They decide:

* What counts as history
* What is erased
* What is remembered
* What is forbidden
* What is patriotic
* What is dangerous

Their power does not come mainly from weapons.

Their power comes from controlling the relationship between memory and reality.

### Core Belief

History is constructed.

If something is not recorded, it does not officially exist.

If something is rewritten, it has always been that way.

### Narrative Role

The Archivist route should feel:

* Formal
* Bureaucratic
* Ritualized
* Authoritative
* Cold
* Monumental

The Archivists are not simply villains.

They believe social order depends on stable narratives.

They may genuinely believe that rewriting history protects society from collapse.

---

## 6.2 Whalemen

Color:

Blue

Role:

Underground resistance.

The Whalemen exist outside the official record.

They preserve:

* Forbidden memories
* Erased names
* Hidden testimonies
* Fragments of lost history
* Evidence of the palace’s contradictions

They are associated with the ocean, depth, sound, memory, and submerged truth.

### Core Belief

Memory survives even when history is erased.

Truth is not always written in official documents.

### Narrative Role

The Whalemen route should feel:

* Hidden
* Intimate
* Fragmented
* Emotional
* Submerged
* Dangerous

The Whalemen are not purely heroic.

They may also manipulate memory, myth, and trauma for their own purposes.

---

# 7. PLAYER POSITION

The player starts without a fixed faction identity.

The player is not immediately told whether to trust the Archivists or the Whalemen.

Instead, the player’s alignment gradually emerges through:

* Items collected
* Spaces explored
* Dice Check outcomes
* Environmental interpretation
* Narrative exposure

The player should feel that they are not simply choosing a faction in a menu.

They are being shaped by the world.

---

# 8. MEMORY VS HISTORY

This is one of the central conceptual conflicts.

## History

In Osaider, history is:

* Official
* Written
* Archived
* Edited
* Authorized
* Institutional

History belongs to the Archivists.

## Memory

Memory is:

* Personal
* Fragmentary
* Emotional
* Unstable
* Hidden
* Embodied

Memory belongs to the Whalemen.

The game should constantly ask:

Is truth what is recorded?

Or is truth what is remembered?

---

# 9. ENVIRONMENTAL STORYTELLING PRINCIPLES

The world should communicate through:

* Documents
* Machines
* Broken rituals
* Repeated symbols
* Locked containers
* Mechanical puzzles
* Spatial contradictions
* Audio fragments
* Item descriptions
* Hidden faction traces

Avoid explaining everything through dialogue.

The player should infer the world through objects and systems.

---

# 10. VISUAL LANGUAGE

## Archivist Visual Direction

Archivist-related spaces may include:

* Red light
* Seals
* Stamps
* Filing systems
* Record cabinets
* Official documents
* Marble or stone surfaces
* Monumental symmetry
* Ceremonial furniture
* Controlled lighting

The feeling should be:

Official truth made architectural.

## Whalemen Visual Direction

Whalemen-related spaces may include:

* Blue light
* Rust
* Water
* Hidden marks
* Pipes
* Submerged chambers
* Scratched messages
* Memory fragments
* Improvised tools
* Organic or whale-like forms

The feeling should be:

Forbidden memory surviving beneath the system.

---

# 11. ITEM DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Items are not only collectibles.

Each item should function as:

* A narrative clue
* A piece of ideological evidence
* A gameplay token
* A possible contribution to Dice Check

An item should answer at least one of these questions:

* Who used this?
* Who erased this?
* Why was this preserved?
* Which faction would care about this?
* What does this reveal about the palace?

# 13. TONE

The tone of Osaider should be:

* Mysterious
* Bureaucratic
* Poetic
* Political
* Mechanical
* Melancholic

It should avoid becoming too direct or explanatory.

The game should feel like reading an archive that is trying to hide something.

---

# 14. AI USAGE NOTES

When generating new worldbuilding content for Osaider:

1. Do not make the truth too explicit.
2. Maintain ambiguity around the war.
3. Keep Archivist and Whalemen morally complex.
4. Use objects and spaces to imply history.
5. Prefer environmental storytelling over exposition.
6. Respect the red Archivist / blue Whalemen color distinction.
7. Keep the palace as both architecture and machine.
8. Avoid generic fantasy language.
9. Use bureaucratic, archival, industrial, and oceanic imagery.
10. Make every new module connect to the core themes of history, memory, and production.
